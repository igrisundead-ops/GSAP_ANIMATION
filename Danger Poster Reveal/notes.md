# Danger Tilt Poster Reveal

## Primary Use Case

A cinematic editorial poster reveal where a hidden subject image sits behind dominant typography, while the entire composition carries a subtle premium tilt effect. The defining behavior is the relationship between the bold text and the partially obscured portrait behind it. The image should feel discovered through the text, not placed beside it.

This works best for character-driven quotes, authority statements, dark creator intros, dramatic hero sections, and strong statement visuals.

## Best Used For

- quote-based cinematic posters
- authority-driven hero intros
- dark editorial landing sections
- founder or character reveal sections
- dramatic statement typography
- premium creator intros
- cinematic title cards
- stylized quote reveals
- narrative opening visuals

## Visual Behavior

The main subject image exists behind the typography and remains partially masked by the text stack. The composition begins with a perspective tilt, giving the entire frame a premium cinematic entrance rather than a flat reveal.

The hidden image enters first with blur reduction and lateral movement. Once the image is established, the large statement text reveals over it in bold vertical rhythm.

Only three words should dominate visually to preserve hierarchy and impact. Supporting lines should never overpower the structure.

The final frame should feel like a high-end editorial poster rather than a standard text layout.

## Motion Sequence

1. The entire composition enters with a premium perspective tilt and controlled settle.
2. The hidden portrait image reveals first using blur reduction, opacity increase, and directional movement.
3. The subject settles behind the text zone.
4. The three dominant words animate in with staggered reveal timing.
5. A subtle ambient tilt remains after the intro to keep the composition alive.

- the image is visually weak or poorly cut out

- the layout is UI-heavy instead of visual-first
- the design requires softness rather than impact
