# Stranger Rejection

## Primary Use Case

A contrast beat that communicates distrust, distance, unfamiliarity, or non-recognition.

## Best Used For

- trust messaging
- social proof narratives
- authority-building campaigns
- brand familiarity storytelling
- sales psychology explainers

## Visual Behavior

A centered portrait enters with cinematic blur resolve. Large outline typography in the background builds weight and emphasis. A red X overlays the subject to symbolize rejection or lack of trust. Text arrives after the visual contrast is established.

## Motion Sequence

1. Oversized outlined words slide into the background
2. Central subject enters from below
3. Red X overlays the face
4. Kicker lands
5. Main headline staggers in

## Emotional Effect

- tension
- rejection
- contrast
- emphasis
- authority

## Works Best With

- founder portraits
- customer avatars
- authority figures
- anti-pattern messaging
- trust narratives

## Avoid Using When

- the message should feel warm and inviting from the start
- the brand avoids strong symbolic cues
- the tone is soft rather than assertive

- X size

This is a strong mid-story contrast beat. It works well after a symbolic opener and before a clarification beat.
