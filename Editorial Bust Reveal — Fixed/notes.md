# Editorial Bust Reveal with Cinematic Entry and Glitch Accent

## Primary Use Case

This animation is a premium editorial poster reveal built for historical figures, cultural campaigns, luxury educational content, and prestige-driven visual storytelling where the hero asset must feel important before the typography even fully appears.

The main bust or statue enters from the lower-left quadrant with a slow cinematic rise, slight rotational tilt, and controlled settling motion. After the object establishes visual authority, the typography reveals with blur-slide-up motion, followed by a restrained premium glitch accent across both the text and the asset to create tension and modern contrast.

This creates a composition that feels both classical and contemporary.

## Visual Behavior

The hero bust begins outside the frame in the lower-left quadrant, slightly rotated and compressed, then rises diagonally upward into its final hero position. The motion feels deliberate and weighted, not fast or decorative.

Once the asset settles, the supporting text enters in layers:
- upper supporting body copy
- highlighted date
- large serif headline
- social footer

Each text block reveals upward with blur reduction and elegant opacity choreography.

After the composition lands, a subtle editorial glitch flicker affects both the typography and the statue. This is not a harsh digital glitch—it is a restrained premium distortion that creates visual tension and keeps the poster from feeling static.

The final frame should feel like a museum poster that has been given cinematic life.

## Motion Sequence

1. The bust begins below and left of the visible frame.
2. The bust rises diagonally upward with slight tilt and slow-fast-slow cinematic easing.
3. Blur resolves as the statue settles into a crisp hero position.
4. A soft light sweep passes across the composition.
5. Supporting copy reveals upward with blur reduction.
6. The highlighted date enters next.
7. Large serif title reveals with staggered blur-slide-up motion.
8. Footer/social details fade into place.
9. A restrained glitch burst affects both title and asset.
10. A second softer glitch pulse reinforces the premium editorial tension.
11. The bust remains with subtle ambient floating motion after lock-in.

## Implementation Notes

The most important part of this animation is the pacing of the asset motion.

The statue should not simply slide in—it must feel heavy and intentional. The slow beginning, faster middle, and soft final settle create the premium cinematic feeling. This is critical.

The glitch must remain restrained. It should feel like editorial tension, not a broken screen effect. Short, elegant RGB split flickers are stronger than aggressive digital distortion.

Typography should remain sharp and controlled. The serif title carries authority, while the supporting copy must stay secondary and elegant.

The final frame should feel like a printed luxury campaign poster that has been given subtle motion, not a noisy motion-graphics sequence.
