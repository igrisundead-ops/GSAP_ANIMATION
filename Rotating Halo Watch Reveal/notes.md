# Rotating Halo Watch Reveal

## Primary Use Case

This animation is a premium object-centered composition built for luxury product storytelling in vertical and square layouts. It is designed to make a single hero object feel deliberate, prestigious, and visually important through restrained motion, centered framing, and clean typography hierarchy.

The rotating dotted halo creates visual focus around the product, while the blur slide-up text reveal establishes emotional context before the viewer fully settles on the object.

## Visual Behavior

The main watch asset sits centered inside a rotating dotted circular halo. The orbit creates controlled motion around the object without overwhelming it, reinforcing focus and precision.

Above the watch, the quote stack enters with blur reduction and upward slide motion. The top script line feels soft and secondary, while the bold main headline anchors the composition visually.

The overall scene remains minimal and spacious, allowing the object, orbit, and typography to carry the emotional weight without visual clutter.

## Motion Sequence

1. The dotted halo softly fades in with a subtle scale settle.
2. The watch enters with blur resolve, slight upward correction, scale recovery, and restrained rotation.
3. The top script line reveals upward with soft blur reduction.
4. The bold headline follows with a stronger blur-slide-up motion and sharper final settle.
5. Supporting caption text fades in softly near the bottom.
6. The full composition locks into a premium still state.
7. The dotted orbit continues a slow elegant rotation around the object.
8. Optional micro-floating motion keeps the watch subtly alive without distracting from the composition.

## Implementation Notes

The dotted orbit must rotate slowly enough to feel intentional and premium, never like a loading spinner. It should read as framing, not utility.

The watch motion should be restrained. No heavy bounce or exaggerated overshoot. Small blur resolution and controlled settle create a much stronger luxury feel than loud movement.

Typography hierarchy is critical:
- script line = emotional support
- bold headline = visual anchor

The final still frame should feel like a luxury editorial poster with just enough motion to feel alive.

Spacing matters heavily. Too much compression weakens the premium feel. Breathing room is part of the design.
