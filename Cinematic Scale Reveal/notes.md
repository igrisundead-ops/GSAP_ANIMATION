# Game Over Cinematic Scale Reveal

## Primary Use Case

This animation is a cinematic character-led poster reveal built for bold vertical compositions where both the typography and the hero subject need to arrive with weight, presence, and controlled intensity.

It is designed for moments where the frame should feel like a premium campaign poster coming to life: the text lands large and dominant, the character scales into importance, and then the typography exits elegantly so the viewer is left with the subject as the lasting focal point.

## Best Used For

- character intro posters
- cinematic vertical hero reveals
- campaign teaser visuals
- fashion or streetwear promotional motion
- dystopian or dramatic story ads
- premium short-form poster animations
- title-card intros for reels and story formats
- bold brand statements with one central figure
- high-contrast visual hooks for short-form content

## Visual Behavior

The composition begins with oversized headline typography and a central hero figure in a dramatic vertical frame. Both the text and the subject scale upward into place, creating a feeling of escalation and arrival.

After the viewer has registered the title, the typography animates out of view in a sleek upward motion with blur and fade, leaving the subject alone in the frame. This creates a premium transition from title-led composition to image-led composition.

The overall result feels like a cinematic poster reveal with an Apple-like sense of restraint: bold, clean, smooth, and expensive rather than chaotic or over-animated.

## Motion Sequence

1. The hero image begins slightly smaller, lower in frame, and softly blurred.
2. The headline begins scaled down with slight blur and reduced opacity.
3. The hero scales up and settles into place with blur resolve and a smooth vertical correction.
4. The headline scales up and sharpens into full visibility.
5. Supporting accent graphics, such as the orange circle, resolve into the composition.
6. The frame holds briefly so both the message and subject can register.
7. The typography then animates upward and out of view with a clean blur fade.
8. Accent graphics can exit with the text in the same motion family.
9. The hero continues a subtle scale drift or micro-float, keeping the scene alive after the text has left.

## Implementation Notes

The key to this animation is the handoff between typography and subject. The text must arrive with authority, but it should not overstay. Its exit is part of the emotional payoff.

The scale-up motion should feel smooth and premium, not like a zoom effect thrown on top. Blur should resolve cleanly into sharpness. The hold duration before the text exits matters a lot, since it gives the eye time to absorb the message before attention is redirected to the subject.

The final text exit should be sleek, light, and deliberate. It should feel like the composition is clearing space for the hero rather than simply removing elements.

If accent shapes are used, they should support the composition and typography, not become independent focal points.
